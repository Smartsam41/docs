## Welcome to CoreX WMS

CoreX is a modern warehouse management system designed from the ground up for third-party logistics (3PL) providers. Whether you're running a single-site operation or managing multiple facilities, CoreX gives your team the tools to receive inbound freight, store and track inventory, fulfill outbound orders at scale, and invoice your shipper clients accurately — all from one platform.

CoreX is built around the real workflows your team runs every day: scanning cartons at the dock, picking orders in waves, packing to the right box, rating and printing carrier labels, and closing out shipments with a manifest. Every screen is designed to reduce clicks, surface the right information at the right moment, and keep your floor moving.

## How CoreX is organized

CoreX separates concerns cleanly so each team in your warehouse sees what they need:

- **Operations staff** (receivers, pickers, packers) work through scan-driven mobile flows — scan a barcode, confirm the action, move on. No training required beyond the basics.
- **Supervisors and managers** use wave planning, task queues, and the Operations Control Center to keep throughput on track.
- **Account managers** manage shipper accounts, rate cards, and invoices from the Customers module.
- **Warehouse administrators** configure the system — locations, rules, carriers, integrations, and team permissions — from the Admin panel.

## Key concepts

**Accounts** — Every shipper (your client) is set up as an Account in CoreX. Inventory, orders, billing, and reporting are all scoped to an account, so you can serve dozens of clients from the same warehouse without commingling data.

**Receiving Orders** — An inbound shipment is tracked as a Receiving Order (RO). It links a Purchase Order or ASN to an expected arrival, dock appointment, and receiving workflow.

**Shipping Orders** — An outbound shipment is tracked as a Shipping Order (SO). Orders flow from channel integrations or manual entry into wave planning, then through pick → pack → label → ship.

**Waves** — A wave is a batch of shipping orders released to the floor for picking together. Waves can be built manually or auto-generated by CoreX based on cutoff times, carrier deadlines, and pick path optimization.

**Locations** — Every storage position in your warehouse is a named location in CoreX. Locations have types (receive, storage, stage, pack, dock), zone assignments, and capacity rules.

**Rate Cards** — Each shipper account has a rate card defining what you charge for receiving, storage, pick/pack handling, and outbound shipping. Billable events are captured automatically and rolled into invoices.

<Note>
  CoreX is a multi-portal system. Your team logs in through the **Operations Portal**. Your shipper clients have their own read-only **Seller Portal** where they can check inventory levels, order status, and download invoices.
</Note>
