## Operations Control Center

Navigate to **Reports → Operations Control** for the real-time operational dashboard.

The Operations Control Center is designed to be displayed on a large screen in your warehouse supervisor's station. It shows:

**Receiving panel:**
- Trucks currently at dock / expected today
- ROs in progress — % complete, receiver assigned
- Receiving discrepancy count today
- Units received today vs. target

**Shipping panel:**
- Orders by status (Ready, Waved, Picking, Packing, Staged, Shipped)
- Carrier cutoff countdown timers — color-coded green/yellow/red
- Active wave count and pick completion rates
- Exceptions requiring attention

**Inventory panel:**
- Empty forward pick locations count (critical — action needed)
- Open replenishment tasks
- Inventory accuracy rate (from most recent cycle counts)
- Lots expiring within 7 days

**Labor panel:**
- Headcount on floor by function (receiving, picking, packing, replenishment)
- Units per hour by function vs. standard
- Clock-in status by employee

The dashboard refreshes every 60 seconds automatically. Individual panels can be pinned or hidden based on what's relevant to your operation.

## SLA Compliance

Navigate to **Reports → SLA Compliance**.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Cutoff compliance">
    Measures what percentage of orders with a same-day ship commitment actually shipped by the carrier cutoff. Break down by:
    - Account
    - Carrier and service level
    - Day of week (to identify problem patterns — e.g., Monday is always bad due to weekend order backlog)
    - Order source channel
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="On-time receipt SLA">
    Measures your receiving throughput SLA — how quickly orders are received and put away after truck arrival. Common 3PL commitments: all inbound receipts processed within 24 hours of arrival (or 48 hours for large freight).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Order processing time">
    Time from order import to shipped status. Break down into components:
    - Order-to-wave time (how long until the order was waved)
    - Wave-to-pick complete time
    - Pick-to-pack time
    - Pack-to-ship time

    Identifying which stage has the most lag helps you direct improvement efforts.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Delivery performance (carrier)">
    Carrier-reported on-time delivery rates from tracking data. How often does your carrier deliver on the committed date? Compare across carriers to support contract negotiations.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

Configure SLA targets per account from the account's detail page (**Customers → Accounts → [Account] → SLA Settings**). CoreX then shows red/green/yellow indicators in SLA reports based on performance vs. target.

## Labor Productivity

Navigate to **Reports → Labor Productivity**.

### Standard rates

First, configure **Labor Standards** (**Admin → Labor Standards**) — expected throughput rates for each operation type:

| Operation | Example standard |
|-----------|----------------|
| Receiving | 80 cartons per hour |
| Putaway (pallet) | 15 pallets per hour |
| Picking (each) | 120 units per hour |
| Packing (small parcel) | 30 cartons per hour |
| Cycle counting | 200 locations per hour |

### Productivity report

The Labor Productivity report shows:
- **Units per hour** (or cartons, pallets, orders — per operation) for each employee
- **Performance vs. standard** — % above or below the expected rate
- **Total hours worked** vs. **productive hours** (time actually performing tasks vs. idle/support time)
- **By shift** — compare productivity across morning, afternoon, and evening shifts

Use this data for:
- Performance coaching and reviews
- Incentive program tracking (if you pay productivity bonuses)
- Capacity planning (if you know your pick rate, you can staff waves appropriately)

### Shift Summary report

**Reports → Shift Summary** gives a one-page summary of everything that happened in a shift:
- Units received, orders shipped, orders picked, orders packed
- Headcount and total labor hours
- Exceptions opened and resolved
- SLA compliance for the shift
- Comparison to prior week same-day

Shift summaries can be emailed automatically to supervisors at the end of each shift.

## Carrier Quote History

Navigate to **Reports → Carrier Quote History** to see all rated shipments:

- Every rate request made at pack time
- All carrier/service combinations quoted
- The rate selected and the reason (cheapest, preferred, rule-forced)
- Actual carrier charge (if carrier invoice audit is configured)

Use this report to:
- Audit whether your carrier rules are firing correctly
- Identify savings opportunities if a consistently cheaper carrier is consistently losing to a preferred carrier
- Identify shipments where actual carrier charges differ from quoted rates (carrier billing disputes)

## Report Generator

Navigate to **Reports → Report Generator** to build custom reports on any data in CoreX.

The report generator lets you:
1. **Choose your data source** — Shipping Orders, Receiving Orders, Inventory, Billing Transactions, Cycle Counts, etc.
2. **Select fields** — choose which columns to include in the report
3. **Apply filters** — filter by date range, account, status, item, carrier, etc.
4. **Group and aggregate** — group by account, date, SKU, etc. and sum or average numeric fields
5. **Sort** — sort by any column
6. **Save and schedule** — save the report configuration and optionally schedule it to run and email automatically

Custom reports can be exported to CSV or PDF on demand. Scheduled reports can be emailed daily, weekly, or monthly to any CoreX user or external email address.

<Tip>
  Save your most-used custom reports as **Favorites** for quick access from the Reports home page. Shared reports (visible to all users with report access) can be created by Admins and Managers.
</Tip>

## Seller Portal analytics

Your shipper clients see a limited analytics view in their **Seller Portal**:
- Their own order status and shipping history
- Inventory levels and lot aging
- Invoice history and billing summary
- SLA performance (your on-time rate against commitments to them)

You control which data is visible in the Seller Portal from **Admin → Branding → Seller Portal Settings**. Giving shippers self-service visibility reduces inbound questions and builds trust.
