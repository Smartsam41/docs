CoreX's invoicing module turns the billable events captured during warehouse operations into accurate, professional invoices. The process is designed for speed: at billing time, all events are already collected and organized — you review, adjust if needed, finalize, and send.

## Viewing invoices

Navigate to **Billing → Invoices** to see all invoices. Filter by:
- **Account** — view invoices for a specific shipper
- **Status** — Draft, Finalized, Sent, Paid, Overdue, Disputed
- **Billing period** — filter by invoice period date range
- **Invoice number** — search for a specific invoice

Click any invoice to open the detail view, which shows all line items, the billable events that back each line, and the payment status.

## Generating an invoice

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Billing → Invoices">
    Click **Generate Invoice**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the account and billing period">
    Choose:
    - **Account** — which shipper to invoice
    - **Billing period start and end date** — the period to invoice. CoreX will include all unbilled transactions with an event date within this range.

    CoreX shows a **preview** of what will be included: total transaction count, estimated total, and a breakdown by charge category.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and generate">
    Click **Generate**. CoreX creates a **Draft** invoice containing:
    - All billable event transactions for the period, rolled up by charge type
    - Storage charges calculated for the period
    - Any manually added billing transactions
    - Applied minimum charges (if the subtotal is below the account's monthly minimum)
    - Applicable taxes (if configured)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the draft">
    Open the draft invoice and review:
    - **Summary section** — total by charge category
    - **Line items** — each charge line with quantity, rate, and amount
    - **Transaction detail** — click any line item to see the individual events that make up that line (e.g., click "Pick Fees" to see each order's pick charge)

    If anything looks wrong, you can:
    - Edit a line item's quantity or rate (with an audit note)
    - Add a manual credit or additional charge
    - Remove a specific transaction from this invoice and move it to the next period
  </Step>
  <Step title="Finalize the invoice">
    When the invoice is correct, click **Finalize**. The invoice is locked — no further edits without voiding and regenerating. All transactions included in this invoice are marked as **Billed** so they won't appear on the next invoice.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send to the shipper">
    Click **Send Invoice**. Options:
    - **Email** — send a PDF of the invoice to the account's billing contact email(s)
    - **Seller Portal** — make it visible in the shipper's Seller Portal (always available; email is optional)
    - **Download PDF** — download the invoice PDF to send via your own process

    The invoice status changes to **Sent**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Invoice statuses

| Status | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Draft** | Invoice generated but not finalized. Editable. |
| **Finalized** | Locked and ready to send. Not yet delivered to the shipper. |
| **Sent** | Delivered to the shipper via email or portal. |
| **Paid** | Payment confirmed. |
| **Overdue** | Past payment due date and not paid. |
| **Disputed** | Shipper has raised a dispute on one or more line items. |
| **Voided** | Invoice cancelled. All included transactions are returned to unbilled status. |

## Billing transactions

Navigate to **Customers → Billing Transactions** (or from an account's detail page → **Billing** tab) to see the raw event-level detail.

Each billing transaction record shows:
- **Event type** — what happened (receive, pick, pack, store, etc.)
- **Event date** — when it occurred
- **Reference** — the linked receiving order, shipping order, or other document
- **Quantity** — how many units, pallets, orders, etc.
- **Rate** — the rate applied from the rate card
- **Amount** — quantity × rate
- **Status** — Unbilled, Billed (on a specific invoice), or Credited

### Filtering and exporting transactions

Use the filter tools to narrow transactions by:
- Date range
- Charge type
- Status (unbilled only, to see what will be on the next invoice)
- Reference document

Export any transaction view to CSV for reconciliation or audit purposes.

## Handling disputes

When a shipper disputes an invoice:

1. Navigate to the invoice → click **Mark as Disputed**.
2. Select the disputed line items and enter the dispute reason.
3. The invoice moves to **Disputed** status.

To resolve:
- **Issue a credit memo** — create a credit for the disputed amount and apply it to the account's next invoice
- **Void and reissue** — void the invoice, adjust the transactions, and regenerate
- **Resolve with no change** — document the outcome and mark the dispute as resolved if the charge was correct

Credit memos are tracked separately and appear in the **Billing Transactions** list as negative amounts.

## Credit memos

Create a credit memo from **Billing → Invoices → New Credit Memo**:
- Select the account
- Enter the credit amount and reason
- Optionally link to the invoice being credited

The credit memo is applied automatically to the account's next generated invoice, reducing the total due.

## Payment tracking

Mark invoices as paid from the invoice detail page → click **Record Payment**:
- Enter the **payment date**, **payment method** (ACH, check, wire, credit card), and **reference number**
- The invoice moves to **Paid** status
- Payment is recorded in the account's payment history

CoreX does not process payments directly — it tracks payment status. Connect your accounting system (QuickBooks, NetSuite, Xero) via the API or use the export tools to sync invoices and payments.

## Automated invoice generation

For large numbers of accounts, manually generating invoices at the end of each billing period is time-consuming. Use **Scheduled Invoice Generation** (**Admin → System Parameters → Billing**) to have CoreX automatically generate draft invoices on the billing period end date for each account.

The drafts are available for review in **Billing → Invoices** the next morning. Review and finalize before sending.

<Note>
  Automated generation creates **Draft** invoices only. You always have a review step before finalizing and sending. CoreX never sends an invoice automatically without supervisor approval.
</Note>

## Invoice customization

Customize the invoice PDF appearance from **Admin → Branding**:
- Upload your logo
- Set your company name, address, and payment instructions
- Add a custom footer message
- Configure which fields are shown on the invoice (you can hide rate card rate details if preferred)

Invoice PDFs are professional, print-ready documents suitable for emailing to your shipper accounts.

## Accounting integrations

Export invoices and transactions for your accounting system:
- **QuickBooks export** — export finalized invoices in QuickBooks-compatible format
- **CSV export** — export transactions in flat format for any accounting system
- **API** — pull invoice and transaction data via the CoreX REST API for custom integrations

See [API Reference](/integrations/api) for invoice and transaction API endpoints.
